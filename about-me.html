Thesis:

Synonymous codons are not used with equal frequency, and this phenomenon is known as codon bias or codon usage bias. Codon bias has a significant influence on biological processes and can affect the fitness of organisms. Proteins can be divided into regions that can form a stable three-dimensional structure (structural domains, or SDs) and regions that cannot (intrinsically disordered regions, or IDRs). Both SDs and IDRs have important functions, and most proteins have both types of regions. In this study, we investigated the difference in codon bias between IDRs and SDs of orthologous single-copy genes from the human, chimpanzee, and mouse genomes, and analyzed the properties of genes that exhibit different codon bias between IDRs and SDs. We identified 6,114 gene families of orthologous single-copy genes from human, chimpanzee, and mouse genomes, and predicted the IDRs and SDs of coding sequences (CDS) for these genes using SPOT-Disorder. We used a sequence random simulation method to determine if the codon bias of IDRs and SDs was significantly different. Genes that exhibited significantly greater codon bias in IDRs than in SDs were defined as IDRs-strong-codon-bias genes, while those that exhibited significantly less codon bias in IDRs than in SDs were defined as IDRs-weak-codon-bias genes. We alsostudied the conservation of IDRs-strong/weak-codon-bias genes across these species, meaning that three genes of a gene family are all IDRs-strong-codon-bias genes or are all IDRs-weak-codon-bias genes.

Paper:

Flavonoids are a type of polyphenolic substance that are widely distributed in vegetables and fruits. They possess a range of biological activities, including antioxidant, antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties. Different flavonoids have different anticancer activities depending on their structures. To investigate this, we compared the anti-proliferation activity of 20 types of flavonoids using the CCK-8 method and analyzed the structure-function relationship of flavonoids by examining their anti-proliferation activity. This will help us identify the important and efficient structures of flavonoids.
