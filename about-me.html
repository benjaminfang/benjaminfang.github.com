Dissertation

Synonymous codons are not used in equal frequencies, and this phenomenon is termed codon bias or codon usage bias. Codon bias has significant influences on biological processes and then influences the fitness of organisms. According to the region of proteins whether can form a stable three-dimensional structure, regions of proteins can be divided into intrinsically disordered regions(IDRs) and structural domains(SDs). Both IDRs and SDs have important functions, and most proteins have both SDs and IDRs. We studied the difference of codon bias between IDRs and SDs of orthologous single-copy genes from human, chimpanzee and mouse genomes, and analyzed properties of genes which codon bias between IDRs and SDs is different. We got 6,114 gene families of orthologous single- copy genes of human, chimpanzee and mouse, and predicted IDRs and SDs of coding sequence(CDS) of these genes by using SPOT-Disorder. Using sequence random simulation method, we can judge if the codon bias of IDRs and SDs is significantly different. Genes which codon bias of IDRs portion is significantly great than codon bias of SDs portion were defined as IDRs strong codon bias genes, and genes which codon bias of IDRs portion is significantly less than codon bias of SDs portion were defined as IDRs weak codon bias genes. We also studied the conservation of IDRs strong/weak codon bias genes across these species, that is, three genes of a gene family are all IDRs strong codon bias genes or are all IDRs weak codon bias genes.
